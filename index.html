Concept

Flesh & Code is a hybrid philosophical‑artistic project: 320 aphorisms (“arrows”) inspired by Nietzsche and Merleau‑Ponty, translated into images, loops, audio and 3D objects. It redefines digital ontology through art, philosophy and technology.

Philosophical roots: phenomenology, hermeneutics, glitch aesthetics.

Artistic approach: stills, animated loops, bilingual voice and physical 3D materialisations.

Global ambition: from Guadalajara to international festivals such as MUTEK, Ars Electronica and Transmediale.

Fragments

320 poetic “arrows” provoke reflection on embodiment, perception, glitch, time and digital creation.

Essays & Manifesto

Essays expand on Encroachment, Passivity, Chiasm and Creation. A 21‑clause manifesto and vows guide digital creators, curators and audiences.

Toolkit

The phenomenology toolkit offers five axes – Hybrid Skin, Composed Latency, Feedback Compose, Temporal Plasticity and Critical Surfacing – with exercises for artists.

Objects

3D‑printed sculptures materialise concepts like the Undo Key, QR‑Rune Tablet and Wi‑Fi Bone – reminders that code extends into flesh.

Project Timeline & Impact

  • 2025

    Research & Development: Finalise aphorisms, develop the phenomenology toolkit and commence prototype designs.

  • Early 2026

    Production: Render still images, record bilingual voiceovers, compose music and build the interactive kiosk.

  • Mid 2026

    Exhibition Premiere: Install the work in Guadalajara with live performances, immersive rooms and object displays.

  • Late 2026

    Circulation: Share the work at international festivals such as Ars Electronica, Transmediale and MUTEK, and release digital editions.

Impact Goals

  • Offer a new digital ontology that is both poetic and practical for artists and thinkers.
  • Provoke embodied reflection about technology not as abstraction but as lived flesh.
  • Position Guadalajara as a hub for cutting‑edge philosophical art practice.
  • Prepare the collection for circulation in festivals, galleries and digital editions.
