About the Project

Flesh and Code is a hybrid philosophical‑artistic project conceived as an ontology in fragments. It merges Nietzschean aphorisms with phenomenological insights and digital aesthetics. Our bodies and technologies are no longer separate — they are porous, entangled and reversible.

Fragments

320 aphoristic “arrows” – lightning‑like poetic fragments – provoke reflection on embodiment, perception, glitch, time and digital creation.

Essays & Manifesto

Essays expand on themes such as Encroachment, Passivity, Chiasm and Creation. A 21‑clause manifesto and five vows guide digital creators, curators and audiences.

Toolkit

A phenomenology toolkit offers five axes – Hybrid Skin, Composed Latency, Feedback Compose, Temporal Plasticity and Critical Surfacing – with exercises for artists.

Objects

3D‑printed sculptures materialise concepts like the Undo Key, QR‑Rune Tablet and Wi‑Fi Bone – reminders that code extends into flesh.

Project Timeline & Impact

  • 2025

    Research & Development: Finalise aphorisms, develop phenomenology toolkit and commence prototype designs.

  • Early 2026

    Production: Render still images, record bilingual voiceovers, compose music and build interactive kiosk.

  • Mid 2026

    Exhibition Premiere: Install the work in Guadalajara with live performances, immersive rooms and object displays.

  • Late 2026

    Circulation: Share the work at international festivals such as Ars Electronica, Transmediale and MUTEK, and release digital editions.

Impact Goals

  • Offer a new ontology that is both poetic and practical for artists and thinkers.
  • Provoke embodied reflection about technology not as abstraction but as lived flesh.
  • Position Guadalajara as a hub for cutting‑edge philosophical art practice.
  • Prepare the collection for circulation in festivals, galleries and digital editions.
